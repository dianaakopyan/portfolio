UX Research · 2022

E-commerce
Checkout Flow

Role

UX Designer

Method

Research + Redesign

Duration

6 weeks

Overview

A checkout flow redesign that increased conversion by 22% in 6 weeks.

An established e-commerce retailer was losing a significant number of users at the checkout stage. The abandonment rate was 71% — well above the industry average of 55–65%. They had tried minor tweaks but conversion hadn't moved. We approached it as a full UX research problem, not a cosmetic fix.

Research

Session recordings revealed the exact moments users gave up.

We analysed 2,400 session recordings and identified 6 critical drop-off points. The biggest: forced account creation (38% of abandonments), unclear shipping costs revealed only at the final step (27%), and a confusing address form that required re-entry on mobile. We also ran 12 moderated usability tests to validate our findings.

Solution

Three changes. One clear principle: remove friction at every step.

We introduced guest checkout as the default path, moved shipping cost estimation to the cart page, and rebuilt the address form with smart autocomplete and inline validation. We also reduced the checkout from 5 steps to 3 by merging related fields and removing unnecessary data collection.
