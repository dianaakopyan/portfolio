Overview

End-to-end design of a health tracking app — from zero to launch on both platforms.

A health-tech startup approached me to design their first mobile product. They had strong backend infrastructure and medical partnerships, but no design team. My role covered everything: research, product strategy, visual design, and handoff to engineering.

Challenge

Health data is complex. Most apps either oversimplify or overwhelm.

Users needed to log dozens of daily metrics without feeling burdened. At the same time, the data needed to be rich enough to be medically useful. We spent the first 6 weeks in research — benchmarking 14 competitor apps, interviewing 24 potential users, and working with 3 medical consultants to understand what data actually mattered.

Design System

A single component library for iOS and Android, built in Figma.

To ship fast and stay consistent, I built a unified design system with 120+ components adapted for both platforms. We followed platform conventions (HIG for iOS, Material 3 for Android) while keeping the brand identity intact across both. This saved approximately 30% of engineering time during implementation.
